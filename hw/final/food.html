FOOD & DRINK

Boston has a wide variety of dining options to satisy any craving. From classic Italian to seafood to dessert, here are my top recommendations for restaurants you have to try:

Union Oyster House

Boston is known for it's seafood, and Union Oyster House is a popular tourist destination for trying regional New Egnland speacialties in a historic setting on the Freedom Trail. Union Oyster House is the oldest restaurant in Boston and the oldest restaurant in continuous service in the U.S., and has received many awards for their food.

Mike's Pastry

The North End is Boston's Little Italy, featuring sites like the Old North Church and the Paul Revere house. Get authentic Italian food and definitely don't skip dessert at the renowned Mike's Pastry bake shop, famous for their delicious cannolis.

Time Out Market Fenway

Boston's innovative eating and drinking destination features the city's top chefs, most talked about restuarants and buzziest bars-plus the latest in arts and culture-all together under one roof. It also is neighbors with the iconic Fenway Park, so grab a bite to eat and check out a baseball game.

Top of the Hub at Prudential

Soaring 52 floors above the Back Bay, Top of the Hub's award winning cuisine, service and ambiance of comfortable sophistication blend with the serenity of Boston's best skyline views to deliver a tryly one-of-a-kind dining experience. It is located at the top of Prudential Tower, heart of Boston's Back Bay.

Faneuil Hall Marketplace

Located in downtown Boston, steps away from the waterfront, Faneiul Hall is an iconic destination at the top of the list of things to see in Boston. You can enjoy unique, locally loved and nationally recognized shops while indulging in the worldwide cuisine at the many restaurants, pubs, and in the world-famous Quincy Market Colonnade.
